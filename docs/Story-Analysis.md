## I. Worldview Overview

### 1.1 The Fall

The zombie virus erupted one week ago. Civilization collapsed overnight—cities reduced to ruins, survivors struggling to stay alive in a wasteland overrun by the undead. The virus is extremely infectious—being attacked by zombies accumulates Infection Value; once it reaches 100%, the human body mutates completely and irreversibly.

But this apocalypse was not without warning. The existence of the bio-research institute, the pristine condition of the aerospace base, the towering castle... all signs suggest that certain factions were already prepared before the outbreak.

### 1.2 Zombie Ecology

The zombies in this post-apocalyptic world are not a monolithic horde of shambling corpses—they have evolved into 16 distinct variants:

**Basic Variants** (Low-risk zones):
- **Wandering Zombie** — The most common type, slow-moving and low attack power
- **Rotting Corpse** — More decomposed, higher attack power but more fragile
- **Crawler** — Zombies that have lost their legs, crawling on the ground with surprising agility

**Evolved Variants** (Medium-risk zones):
- **Sprinting Zombie** — Retained the ability to run, extremely fast, screams to attract companions
- **Bloated Corpse** — Massive body, slow but with extremely high HP, explodes on death
- **Bursting Corpse** — Accumulated flammable gas inside, violently explodes when killed
- **Military/Police Zombie** — Bulletproof vest-wearing variant, attacks have corrosive effects
- **Swarm Mother** — Can summon small zombies, not strong in combat but extremely persistent
- **Armored Zombie** — Entire body covered in hardened keratin, nearly immune to normal attacks

**Advanced Variants** (High-risk zones):
- **Frost Zombie** — Extremely low body temperature variant, attacks inflict freezing effects
- **Tearing Berserker** — Mindless berserker, extremely high attack power with blind and corrosion effects
- **Acid-Spraying Zombie** — Can spray acid at range
- **Leaping Zombie** — Retained jumping ability, swift attacks

**Ultimate Variants** (Inferno zones):
- **Giant Tank Zombie** — Tank-sized, terrifying HP and attack power
- **Phantom Stealth Zombie** — Can turn invisible, extremely high evasion rate
- **Piercing Zombie** — Attacks inflict piercing and corrosion, nearly impossible to defend against

The evolution of these zombies suggests the virus is not mutating naturally—someone is pushing it forward from behind the scenes.

### 1.3 Social Reconstruction

After the apocalypse, survivors spontaneously formed three distinct social structures:

| Social Structure | Representative Faction | Core Philosophy | Mode of Operation |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| Democratic Mutual Aid | Haven Outpost | Unity, equality, mutual help | Collective decision-making, distribution by need |
| Hierarchical Authoritarian | Doom Castle | Order, hierarchy, control | Noble privileges, resource monopoly |
| Independent Autonomy | The Island | Commerce, freedom, neutrality | Royal Coins economy, investment and trade |

Each of these social structures has its strengths and its dark sides. Haven Outpost appears equal but lacks resources; the Castle appears orderly but is rigidly hierarchical; The Island appears free but depends on the Castle's economy.

## II. Region Analysis

### 2.1 Safe Zones

#### 🏠 Home (Survivor Tent/Base)

The player's initial shelter, evolving from a crude tent to a Level 5 base, representing the transformation from "barely surviving" to "truly living." Base upgrades are not just numerical improvements—they symbolize putting down roots in the apocalypse. When you have a real home, you're no longer just passing through.

#### 🌅 Haven Outpost

The word "Haven" carries the weight of the last hope in the apocalypse. The Outpost was single-handedly built by the Outpost Leader, sheltering survivors like Miss V, Su Xiaohan, Lili, and Mu Miaomiao. There is no hierarchy here—everyone is an equal member.

But the Outpost's "equality" comes at a price—resources are never enough, and everyone scrambles to survive. Working is limited to 4 times a day at most; begging is an act of desperation. The Outpost Leader, though respected, is himself exhausted—his sister was "taken away," his old friend became an enemy, and the recorder pen holds evidence of betrayal...

**The Outpost's Core Contradiction**: Equal but impoverished, free but dangerous.

#### 🏰 Doom Castle

A castle that still stands tall in the apocalypse, its towering turrets and thick walls speaking of pre-apocalyptic glory. Inside, the lights burn bright, nobles in fine clothes clink glasses as if the end of the world never arrived.

The Castle's 6-tier nobility system (Noble → Viscount → Earl → Marquis → Duke → Crown Prince) is an elaborate ladder of power:

| Title | How to Obtain | Privileges |
|------|----------|------|
| Noble | Pay with Cigarettes | Enter the Castle, attend banquets |
| Viscount | The King's Quest 1 (10 glucose solutions) | Better medical care, stipend |
| Earl | The King's Quest 2 (10 bottles of high-proof liquor) | Official doctor treatment, Castle employment |
| Marquis | The King's Quest 3 (10 Cigarettes + 5 Omni-Beer + 5 Power Beer) | Patrol duties, attending physician |
| Duke | The King's Quest 4 (3 universal syringes) | Sign documents and attend meetings, court physician |
| Crown Prince | The King's Quest 5 (Assassinate the Outpost Leader) | Castle heir, underground area access |

**The Castle's Core Contradiction**: Orderly but oppressive, wealthy but rotten. The King pursues immortality, building walls of power to keep the apocalypse at bay—but are the people inside those walls truly alive?

### 2.2 Exploration Zones

#### ⛰️ Abandoned Hilltop Watchtower (Easy)

A vantage point; climbing the tower relieves Crash Value. Beside the tower stands a wreath-topped gravestone engraved with the character 「苗」—the grave of Mu Miaomiao's parents. This gravestone is the starting point of Miaomiao's storyline, and the gentlest place in the entire game.

#### 🌾 Abandoned Country Barn (Easy)

A beginner-friendly low-risk map where you can pick fruit. Moldy seeds require the Agriculture Master Badge to plant—a metaphor for "knowledge is power."

#### 🏡 Deep Mountain Farmhouse Village (Easy)

An abandoned rural tourism spot; the caves and vegetable fields still bear traces of pre-apocalyptic life. The fields require the Agriculture Master Badge to use, implying that "knowing how to farm" is a scarce skill in the apocalypse.

#### ⛾ Riverside Campground (Low Risk)

The campground offers the option to scavenge corpses, and there's a foul-smelling tent. Inside the tent lies a recorder pen—a key item for uncovering the grudge between The King and the Outpost Leader. But entering the tent requires the Haven Captain Badge, meaning only those truly trusted by the Outpost can access this piece of history.

#### ⛽ National Highway Service Area (Low Risk)

Ma San's territory. The service area restaurant serves "mystery meat"—a bizarre food that adds +10 Infection Value per serving. This is the entry point to the game's darkest storyline: the truth about Ma San's cannibalism.

#### 🎓 University Town (Low Risk)

Liu Ruyan's alma mater. In the old classrooms, she recognizes you as a senior; the stadium has football zombies; the administration building has a crowbar and car keys. The University Town is the main stage for Liu Ruyan's quest chain, and the place where she recovers her memories from a semi-infected state.

#### ⛽ Abandoned Suburban Gas Station (Low Risk)

Survivor Wang Tiezhu's base—5 Cigarettes for 1 Gasoline. Gasoline is one of the scarcest resources in the game—Liu Ruyan's elopement requires 10, and the yacht requires 1. Wang Tiezhu is one of the rare "tech geeks" in the apocalypse, trading skills for survival.

#### 🏬 Downtown Shopping Mall (Medium Risk)

Where Liu Ruyan first appears. A semi-infected woman huddles in a corner of the mall, still clinging to a shred of consciousness—this is the starting point of Liu Ruyan's storyline. If you don't save her within 30 days, she will turn completely.

#### 🏘️ Old Residential Neighborhood (Medium Risk)

Survivor Old Wolf's base. Old Wolf despises humanity and lives alone—an embodiment of "collapsed trust" in the apocalypse.

#### 🏭 Industrial Park / Processing Factory (Medium Risk)

Industrial ruins; exploring the interior yields building materials.

#### ⚓ Riverside Port Dock (Medium Risk)

Where the Castle yacht is moored. Requires Castle identity + 1 Gasoline to travel to The Island. Enjoying the river view is one of the rare peaceful moments in the apocalypse.

#### 🛒 Chain Warehouse Supermarket (Medium Risk)

Shadow's territory. Shadow is one of the strongest field bosses in the game (400 HP, 50-80 damage, 50% evasion); defeating him yields the GP100 revolver.

#### 🏭 Abandoned Factory Warehouse (Medium Risk)

Old Ma's base—a miserly warehouse guardian with a hilarious conversational style.

#### 🏥 Suburban General Hospital (High Risk)

Where Luluwei resides—a nurse zombie that has retained her sanity. Feeding her canned food raises her Affinity; when maxed, you can bring her home. Luluwei's existence proves one thing: the zombie virus is not irreversible.

#### 🚔 Abandoned Police Station (High Risk)

Searching the evidence room yields weapons and ammunition. The abandonment of the police station symbolizes the total collapse of law and order in the apocalypse.

#### 🎖️ Military Checkpoint (High Risk)

The base of the jittery veteran Veteran Zhao. Veteran Zhao suffers from severe PTSD, representing "psychological trauma" in the apocalypse.

#### 🚇 Underground Subway Tunnel (Desperate)

Delving into the tunnel is one of the most dangerous explorations in the game, but also a way to obtain high-tier supplies. The darkness and unknown of the subway tunnels are the embodiment of apocalyptic fear.

#### 🔬 Bio-Research Institute (Desperate)

Where Dr. Chen is located. The existence of the institute implies the zombie virus may be linked to human research. Dr. Chen requires 50 anti-infection serums before he'll trade the M700 sniper rifle—he is one of the rare "scientists" in the apocalypse, holding the key technology for improved serum.

The Energy Extraction Well is also inside the institute, producing 1 Pure Energy per day. Pure Energy is a key resource for Rocket Mission 2—15 days of waiting is the game's test of "patience."

#### 🚀 Top-Secret Aerospace Base (Inferno)

The most dangerous map in the game, with an encounter rate of up to 90%. The Giant Puppet guards the launch center—who created it? Why is it at the aerospace base? These questions suggest a possible connection between the virus and the aerospace program.

The launch center is the endpoint of the rocket mission chain, and the starting point for three endings.

#### 🕳️ Zombie Nest (Inferno)

The domain of the Zombie King / Silence. 420 HP, 115 damage—this is the strongest regular boss in the game. Deep within the nest, the silent Zombie King awaits challengers. Its name "Silence" suggests a realm beyond life and death.

### 2.3 Hidden Areas

#### 🏝️ The Island (Hidden · Easy)

A hidden map reached via the Castle yacht, with beautiful scenery—it is the fiefdom granted to Duke Yu Mo by The King. The island's residents live in peace, the Castle's flag fluttering at the center—as if the apocalypse never happened.

The Island is the game's "utopia," but it's no true paradise:
- **Karen's King Bar** — A bar invested in by The King, a den of extravagance
- **Elena Queen Street** — A commercial street opened by The Queen, consumerism at its finest
- **Lucius Investment House** — An investment system with gambling mechanics
- **Island Bank** — Established by The Queen, 10% interest, penalty for overdue payments

The three NPCs on the island each have their own stance:
- **Gu Yue** — Fanatically worships The King, representing "blind obedience"
- **Lin Han** — Rationally analyzes The King's strategy, representing "clarity"
- **Duke Yu Mo** — Loyal but lonely island keeper, representing "the price of loyalty"

Duke Yu Mo's quest chain reveals the other side of The Island: the Castle is researching the zombie virus, the research attracted aquatic zombies, and Yu Mo was forced to turn the island into a sea fortress. He was once just an ordinary civil servant who met The King (then still a merchant) at a resort before the apocalypse, and was enfeoffed as a duke to guard the island—an ordinary man thrust into a high position by fate.

## III. Core Character Deep Analysis

### 3.1 The Player (Nameless Survivor)

The player is a survivor with no name and no past. At the start of the game, you are just one of countless fugitives after the zombie virus outbreak. Your identity is defined by your choices—will you become the Castle's Crown Prince, or Haven Outpost's Captain? Will you fly into space alone, or seek hope with your companions?

**The Player's Essence**: A mirror. What you see is not the character's growth, but your own choices in the apocalypse.

### 3.2 Haven Outpost NPCs

#### Miss V — The Ice-Cold Assassin

**Identity**: Female assassin, the coldest member of the Outpost

**Personality**: Cold on the outside, warm on the inside. Daily dialogue is brief and aloof, but as Affinity increases, she gradually reveals her gentler side. She's the type who "says she doesn't care, but her actions say otherwise."

**Function**: Trading system—exchange food/drinks/Cigarettes/Gasoline for random ammunition. The once-daily trade is an important source of ammunition for the Outpost.

**Affinity Stages**: Stranger → Acquaintance → Friend → Close → Soulmate

**Quest Chain** (6 steps, weapon progression):
1. Small Dagger → 2. Knuckle Knife → 3. Dragon Sword → 4. P226 Pistol → 5. UZI Submachine Gun → 6. M4A1 Assault Rifle

From melee to ranged, from cold weapons to firearms, Miss V's quest chain is a complete weapon upgrade path. Her way of trusting you is unique—giving you better weapons so you can survive longer.

**Deep Interpretation**: Miss V's coldness is a product of the apocalypse. She wasn't born cold-blooded—she learned to stop feeling after witnessing too much death. But when she starts giving you better weapons, she's really saying: "I don't want to lose another person."

#### Su Xiaohan — Warmth in the Apocalypse

**Identity**: Nurse girl, supply exchange specialist

**Personality**: Warm, kind, optimistic. A girl who still keeps smiling in the apocalypse, the most healing presence in the Outpost.

**Function**: Trading system—exchange various items for food. Once per day.

**Affinity Stages**: Same as Miss V

**Quest Chain** (6 steps, medical progression):
1. Band-Aid → 2. Surgical Kit → 3. Antibiotics → 4. Anti-Infection Serum → 5. Medical Emergency Kit → 6. Field Medical Case

From the most basic Band-Aid to the top-tier Field Medical Case, Su Xiaohan's quest chain is a complete medical upgrade path. What she gives you isn't weapons—it's the assurance of staying alive.

**Deep Interpretation**: Su Xiaohan's warmth is not naivety—it's a choice. Choosing kindness in the apocalypse takes more courage than choosing coldness. Her smile is the Outpost's most precious resource—not because of its monetary value, but because it reminds everyone that living is about more than just survival.

#### Lili — The Quirky Weapons Girl

**Identity**: Weapons expert, offering recycling/repair/infection removal services

**Personality**: Quirky, mischievous, loves jokes. Cigarettes are her hard currency—recycling weapons earns Cigarettes, repairing guns costs Cigarettes, removing infection costs Cigarettes too.

**Functions**:
- Recycle weapons for Cigarettes
- Repair guns and crossbows
- Remove Infection Value
- Mystery Gift quest

**Affinity Stages**: Same as Miss V

**Deep Interpretation**: Lili is the "businesswoman" of the apocalypse. She trades skills for survival resources—seemingly shrewd, but actually using transactions to maintain human connections. Every trade is a social interaction. Her Cigarette economy is the most stable currency system in the apocalypse.

#### Mu Miaomiao — The Farming Goddess of the Wasteland

**Identity**: Agriculture expert, the Outpost's planting master

**Personality**: Gentle, resilient, reserved. She rarely speaks, but every word carries weight.

**Functions**:
- Buy seeds (1 Cigarette → 3 random seeds)
- Tend the experimental field (Affinity + random seeds)
- Secret Flower Field (Affinity 150+ to unlock, earn Agriculture Master Badge)
- Buried Past quest chain

**Affinity Stages**: Same as Miss V

**Quest Chain** (3 steps, emotional progression):
1. **Picnic Together** — 3 carrots + 3 potatoes, Affinity +20. Simple ingredients, the taste of mom's cooking.
2. **Longing Across Time** — 5 high-proof liquor + 10 rye, Affinity +30. Sweeping the graves of her parents, laying her diary open before the gravestone.
3. **Expand the Experimental Field** — 15 different crops, Affinity +50, earn Field Bow + Arrows ×30. Miaomiao hands you her mentor's bow—"Guard this field, and guard yourself."

**Deep Interpretation**: Mu Miaomiao is the gentlest character in the entire game. Her power isn't weapons, isn't authority—it's the sound of a seed breaking through the soil. Her journey from "couldn't even keep a single seed alive" to "no one will ever go hungry again" is the greatest transformation in the apocalypse. She's no farming goddess—she's just an ordinary person who doesn't want anyone to starve anymore.

**Miaomiao's Diary** is the most tear-jerking item in the game. Dug up from beneath the gravestone next to the watchtower, it records a little girl's story from her parents' death to learning how to grow vegetables. The last page has a drawing of a family holding hands—the wind blows, the pages flutter, as if responding to her.

#### Outpost Leader — The Torn Brother

**Identity**: Founder and leader of Haven Outpost

**Personality**: Pragmatic, resolute, silent. A weathered middle-aged man, his eyes carrying both exhaustion and determination.

**Functions**:
- Dialogue (daily, Affinity +1)
- Gift-giving (daily, Affinity +1)
- Join/leave Haven Outpost
- Receive gift (Affinity maxed, earn Leader's weapon)
- Earn honor (all NPC Affinity maxed, earn Haven Captain Badge + Vector submachine gun)
- Recorder Pen quest chain
- Assassination option (for The King's Quest 5)

**Recorder Pen Quest Chain** (3 steps):
1. **Hand over the recorder pen** → Receive [Letter to Sister], Affinity +10. The Leader learns The King killed his intelligence operative, revealing the grudge between him and The King—The King was once an Outpost member, fell in love with the Leader's sister, and was expelled by the Leader before setting himself up as king.
2. **The Queen's Reply + 5 bottles of liquor** → Affinity +20. The Leader reads the letter and cries while drunk—"Brother, I don't hate you. But some wounds, even time can't heal."
3. **3 universal syringes to save the Leader** → Receive [9×19mm] ×100 + [Token of Love], Affinity +30. The Leader nearly dies from alcohol poisoning; after being saved, he hands you the sunflower pendant The King gave his sister.

**Deep Interpretation**: The Leader is the most tragic character in the entire game. He did everything right—drove away the ambitious, protected his sister, built the Outpost—but the result was losing everyone. His sister went to the Castle and became The Queen, his old friend became his enemy, and the people around him left one by one. His tragedy isn't that he did something wrong—it's that he did everything right and still couldn't stop the tragedy from unfolding.

### 3.3 Castle Faction

#### The King (Karen) — From Merchant to Tyrant

**Identity**: Ruler of Doom Castle, former real estate merchant

**Personality**: Shrewd, suspicious, extremely controlling. From his dialogue, it's clear he relishes power, pursues immortality, and shows no mercy to dissenters.

**The King's Quest Chain** (5 steps, power progression):
1. **The Queen's Troubles** — 10 glucose solutions → Viscount. Seems like concern for The Queen, but is actually the first display of power.
2. **The King's Banquet** — 10 bottles of high-proof liquor → Earl. Using alcohol to win loyalty and maintain rule.
3. **The King's Vices** — 10 Cigarettes + 5 Omni-Beer + 5 Power Beer → Marquis. Begins to reveal his hedonistic nature.
4. **Immortality** — 3 universal syringes → Duke. The obsession with eternal life exposes his fear of death.
5. **Eliminate Dissent** — Assassinate the Outpost Leader → Crown Prince. Finally reveals his tyrant nature—destroying all threats.

**Deep Interpretation**: The King is the embodiment of "order" in the apocalypse, but this order is built on oppression. He was once a member of the Outpost, fell in love with the Leader's sister, and was rejected before setting himself up as king. His lust for power stems from the trauma of rejection—"Since you won't accept me, I'll make you have to look up to me."

He pursues immortality not because he fears death, but because he fears losing power. He sends female spies to infiltrate the Outpost and assassinates the Leader's intelligence operative—these actions reveal his true nature: a man using power to fill an inner void.

**The Truth in the Recorder Pen**: In the foul-smelling tent at the riverside campground lies a recorder pen, containing evidence of The King sending spies to infiltrate the Outpost and assassinating the intelligence operative. This recorder pen is the key item connecting the Castle and Outpost storylines.

#### The Queen (Elena) — The Torn Woman

**Identity**: The Outpost Leader's sister, current Castle Queen

**Personality**: Elegant, enduring, conflicted. She loves The King, but misses her brother. She enjoys the Castle's luxury, but longs for the Outpost's warmth.

**The Queen's Quest Chain** (4 steps):
1. **Drowning Sorrows** — 5 Omni-Beer + 5 Power Beer. The Queen tells the story of the sunflower pendant—it was The King's token of love to her.
2. **The King's Condition** — 10 goji berries. The Queen worries about The King's health; he practices swordplay daily, his body deteriorating.
3. **Sword Practice** — 3 Western swords. The King's paranoia grows worse.
4. **Wedding Anniversary** — 1 M700 sniper rifle. Earn Castle Pass + 40 Royal Coins.

**The Belated Apology**: Deliver the Leader's letter to The Queen—she is the Leader's sister. She writes a reply—"Brother, I don't hate you. But some wounds, even time can't heal."

**Deep Interpretation**: The Queen is the most conflicted character in the entire game. She stands between two walls—one built by The King with power, one held by the Leader with conviction. She is torn apart, and also made whole.

"He only loves me. In this apocalypse, a king who loves only his queen—is that not a kind of romance?" This statement is The Queen's most precise summary of her situation. She knows The King's tyranny, but she also feels his love. She knows her brother is right, but she can never go back.

### 3.4 Island NPCs

#### Duke Yu Mo — The Price of Loyalty

**Identity**: Keeper of The Island, duke enfeoffed by The King

**Background**: Formerly an ordinary civil servant who met The King (then still a merchant) at a resort before the apocalypse, and was enfeoffed as a duke to guard the island.

**Quest Chain** (5 steps):
1. **The Duke's Troubles** — Collect zombie gel to meet quotas
2. **Kingdom Experiment** — Collect 3x zombie gel, launch research project
3. **Sea Monster Landing** — Clear aquatic zombies (research attracted them from the sea)
4. **Island Construction** — Collect building materials to build a sea fortress
5. **Celebration Banquet** — Drink liquor, earn fishing area key

**Affinity Dialogue Progression**:
- Stranger: Loyal to The King
- Acquaintance: The pressure of managing The Island
- Friend: A rare friend, the island holds secrets
- Close: Loneliness, there may be more than just a nuclear power device beneath the island
- Soulmate: The most trusted person, closer than The King

**Deep Interpretation**: Yu Mo is the price of "loyalty." He's no noble, no warrior—just an ordinary man thrust into a high position by fate. He is loyal to The King, but this loyalty makes him lonely—no one on the island truly understands him. When Affinity reaches Soulmate, he says "closer than The King"—this is the deepest betrayal of loyalty, and the truest confession.

#### Gu Yue — The Blind Follower

**Identity**: Island resident, fanatic worshipper of The King

**Personality**: Full of reverence for The King, repeatedly mentioning his greatness across 7 dialogue lines. He represents those in the apocalypse who "blindly follow power"—not out of understanding, but out of a need for faith.

#### Lin Han — The Sober Observer

**Identity**: Island resident, poisonous fish processing expert

**Personality**: Stern-faced, hands scarred from years of handling fish. Across 7 dialogue lines, he rationally analyzes The King's strategy. He represents those in the apocalypse who are "sober but powerless"—seeing through the nature of power but unable to change it.

### 3.5 Map NPCs

#### Ma San — The Cannibal of the Apocalypse

**Identity**: Tyrant at the National Highway Service Area

**Personality**: Rough, vicious, provocative. 15 dialogue lines full of violence and threats.

**Quest Chain** (4 steps, trap ending):
1. Eat mystery meat → Ask Ma San → Hand over 3 Power Beer
2. Beer-braised ribs → Hand over 3 crop side dishes
3. Wants 10 Cigarettes to calm down, promises an armory and RPG
4. Accept the invitation → **Become the dish**; Refuse → Fight

**Deep Interpretation**: Ma San is the extreme embodiment of "the evil of human nature" in the apocalypse. His restaurant serves "mystery meat"—a bizarre food that adds +10 Infection Value per serving. As the quest chain progresses, players discover the true source of that "mystery meat." Ma San's "armory" promise is an elaborate trap—he's not giving you weapons; he's making you his "new dish."

Ma San's existence poses a cruel question: in the apocalypse, where is the line between humanity and bestiality? When you eat that bowl of "mystery meat," you've already crossed it.

#### Liu Ruyan — The Apocalypse's Cinderella

**Identity**: Semi-infected woman, college student

**Personality**: Gentle, strong-willed, independent-minded. When you first meet her at the mall, she still has a shred of consciousness—in 30 days, she'll turn completely.

**Quest Chain** (4 steps, elopement ending):
1. **Old Classroom Memories** → Infirmary, obtain universal syringe. Liu Ruyan recognizes you as a senior—"Before the apocalypse, we might have been schoolmates."
2. **Stadium** → Defeat football zombie
3. **Administration Building** → Use crowbar to open door, obtain car keys
4. **Parking Lot** → 10 Gasoline to start the SUV → **Elopement Ending**

**Deep Interpretation**: Liu Ruyan is the symbol of "hope" in the apocalypse. She was rescued from a semi-infected state, pulled back from the edge of zombification into the human world. Her quest chain is a journey from "waiting to die" to "running away"—ultimately, she chooses to elope with you, driving toward the unknown.

"Before the apocalypse, my biggest wish was a graduation trip." This line is the most heartbreaking in the entire game. In the apocalypse, a simple graduation trip becomes the most extravagant dream. But in the end, you make it happen—with a beat-up SUV, kicking up dust on an abandoned highway.

#### Luluwei — The Lucid Zombie

**Identity**: A nurse zombie that has retained her sanity

**Personality**: Silent, docile. Feeding her canned food raises Affinity; when maxed, you can bring her home.

**Deep Interpretation**: Luluwei's existence proves a crucial fact—the zombie virus is not irreversible. She has retained her sanity, can understand human language, and even brings back medical supplies daily. She is the bridge between humans and zombies, and the key to the "New Force" ending—improved serum can fully restore her to humanity.

#### Dr. Chen — The Prometheus of the Apocalypse

**Identity**: Scientist at the Bio-Research Institute

**Personality**: Rational, driven, lonely. He guards the institute alone, researching improved serum and rocket technology.

**Quest Chain** (3 steps, 3 endings):
1. **Serum Trade** — 50 anti-infection serums → M700 sniper rifle
2. **Rocket Repair** — Defeat the Giant Puppet → Consult about the rocket → Trade The King the AWM for underground key → Dismantle the small nuclear generator
3. **Rocket Launch** — 15 Pure Energy + 10 medical + 10 food + 10 drinks → Launch center, choose 1 of 3

**Deep Interpretation**: Dr. Chen is the Prometheus of the apocalypse—he brought fire (rocket technology) to humanity, but at enormous sacrifice. He can choose to fly into space alone, let the doctor go while he stays, or take his companions along. Whichever choice is made, it means saying goodbye.

In the "Stay Behind" ending, the doctor boards the rocket alone, turns back and says: "Thank you. Live well... for me." This is the most moving farewell in the entire game.

#### Zombie King / Silence — Beyond Life and Death

**Identity**: Ultimate boss deep within the Zombie Nest

**Stats**: 420 HP, 115 damage, 25% evasion

**Deep Interpretation**: The name "Silence" comes from the Buddhist concept of nirvana—a realm beyond life and death. The Zombie King is not the most ferocious zombie, but the most "transcendent" one. It sits silently in the depths of the nest, not attacking proactively, just waiting. Its existence implies that at the end of the apocalypse, life and death no longer matter.

#### Giant Puppet — The Manufactured Tool

**Identity**: Gatekeeper boss of the Aerospace Base

**Stats**: 380 HP, 105 damage, 20% evasion

**Deep Interpretation**: The word "Puppet" implies it was manufactured—not a naturally mutated zombie, but a human-controlled tool. Who created it? Why does it guard the aerospace base? These questions point to a disturbing answer: the zombie virus may not be a natural disaster, but a man-made one.

### 3.6 Other NPCs

#### Wang Tiezhu — The Apocalypse's Tech Geek

**Identity**: Survivor at the abandoned suburban gas station

**Personality**: Tech geek, transactional style. 5 Cigarettes for 1 Gasoline, the only regular source of Gasoline.

**Deep Interpretation**: Wang Tiezhu represents the idea that "skills equal survival" in the apocalypse. He can't fight, can't farm, but he can fix things and refine fuel—in the apocalypse, these skills are worth more than guns.

#### Old Wolf — The Trust-Collapsed Survivor

**Identity**: Lone survivor in the old residential neighborhood

**Personality**: Vigilant, misanthropic. 5 dialogue lines full of distrust toward humanity.

**Deep Interpretation**: Old Wolf is the embodiment of "collapsed trust" in the apocalypse. He wasn't born hating people—he was betrayed too many times. His presence reminds the player: in the apocalypse, the most dangerous thing isn't necessarily zombies—the human heart is the greatest variable.

#### Veteran Zhao — The PTSD Veteran

**Identity**: Jittery veteran at the military checkpoint

**Personality**: Nervous, obvious PTSD symptoms. 5 dialogue lines full of war trauma.

**Deep Interpretation**: Veteran Zhao represents "psychological trauma" in the apocalypse. His PTSD didn't come from zombies—it came from war, meaning that even before the zombie virus outbreak, humans were already hurting each other.

#### Old Ma — The Miser

**Identity**: Guardian of the abandoned factory warehouse

**Personality**: Miserly, comedic. 5 dialogue lines in a lighthearted and humorous style.

**Deep Interpretation**: Old Ma is one of the rare "comic relief characters" in the apocalypse. His presence reminds the player: even in the darkest moments, don't forget to laugh.

## IV. Faction Dynamics and Power Struggles

### 4.1 Three-Way Balance

```
          Doom Castle (The King)
          ╱    Power    ╲
        ╱              ╲
  Haven Outpost (Leader) —— The Island (Yu Mo)
      Conviction    Trade
```

**Castle vs. Outpost**: This is the game's core conflict. The King and the Leader were once "on the same side"—The King fell in love with the Leader's sister, was rejected, and set himself up as king. The two went from allies to enemies, from friends to foes. The Castle represents order and oppression; the Outpost represents freedom and poverty.

**The Island's Stance**: The Island nominally belongs to the Castle (a fiefdom granted to Yu Mo by The King), but in practice maintains a degree of independence. The Island Bank was established by The Queen, and the commercial street is named after her—but the island residents' attitudes toward The King are not uniform (Gu Yue worships, Lin Han is clear-eyed).

### 4.2 Identity Exclusivity Mechanism

The game features an ingenious identity exclusivity system:

- **Holding a Castle ID Card** → Miss V / Su Xiaohan / Lili refuse to talk to you ("You're with the Castle!")
- **Holding a Haven Badge** → Castle guards are hostile, unable to obtain Noble status
- **Holding a Haven Captain Badge** → Permanently unable to obtain any Castle identity
- **Holding a Crown Prince ID Card** → The Outpost Leader expels you

This means the player must choose between two paths—either the Castle path (Power Addiction ending), the Outpost path (Shining Bright ending), or neither (New Force / Space / Hope endings, etc.).

### 4.3 The Recorder Pen—The Hidden Thread Connecting Two Paths

The recorder pen is one of the most critical items in the entire game, connecting the seemingly parallel Castle and Outpost storylines:

1. Find the recorder pen in the foul-smelling tent at the riverside campground (requires Haven Captain Badge)
2. Give the recorder pen to the Outpost Leader → Reveals The King's assassination of the intelligence operative → Receive [Letter to Sister]
3. Give the letter to The Castle Queen → The Queen writes a reply → Receive [The Queen's Reply]
4. Give the reply to the Outpost Leader → The Leader cries while drunk → Receive [Token of Love]
5. Give the Token of Love to The Queen → Unlock the "Love and Hate" ending

This hidden thread is the game's most brilliant design—it lets the player shuttle between two hostile factions, becoming the only person who knows the complete truth.

## VII. Twelve Endings Deep Interpretation

### Ending One: Death 💀

> "Those unfulfilled dreams, unkept promises, unspoken words... all turned to dust with your death."

**Trigger Condition**: HP reaches zero or Infection Value reaches 100

**Interpretation**: The most basic ending, and the most realistic one. In the apocalypse, most people die this way—no last words, no farewells, just silently disappearing. This ending reminds us: being alive is itself a miracle.

**Endgame**: Can only restart.

### Ending Two: An Unknown Future 🌅

> "Nine hundred and ninety-nine days and nights. From the survivor cowering in a tent, you became the most resilient being in this wasteland."

**Trigger Condition**: Survive 999 days

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "perseverance." 999 days means you endured countless bouts of hunger, infection, breakdown, and combat—but you survived them all. The Dimensional Storage Backpack (68 slots) is your reward for persistence—it doesn't belong to this world, yet can hold far more than what's physically possible, implying your existence has transcended the rules of the apocalypse.

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving.

### Ending Three: Power Addiction 👑

> "Power is like a poison, slowly eroding your soul. The King will eventually grow old. And you... are you ready to become the next him?"

**Trigger Condition**: Obtain the Crown Prince ID Card (complete The King's Quest 5: Assassinate the Outpost Leader)

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "power's corruption." You killed the Outpost Leader for power and became the Castle's heir. But power is a poison—you begin indulging in luxury, forgetting who you once were. The final line of this ending is the most terrifying prophecy: "Are you ready to become the next him?"—you are becoming the very person you once opposed.

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving.

### Ending Four: Shining Bright 🌅

> "In the apocalypse, you are the warmest light."

**Trigger Condition**: Obtain the Haven Captain Badge (all Outpost NPC Affinity maxed + granted by the Leader)

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "devotion." You earned every Outpost member's trust with sincerity and became the Haven Vanguard Captain. But this ending has its concerns too—"Tomorrow, you must continue leading everyone to scavenge supplies, fortify defenses, and rescue survivors." You became the light, but light burns itself to shine.

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving.

### Ending Five: Becoming a New Force ⚔️

> "The old forces will eventually fall, and a new legend is being written. And you are the protagonist of this legend."

**Trigger Condition**: Simultaneously meet 6 conditions (rescue Luluwei + rescue Liu Ruyan + defeat Ma San + defeat Shadow + defeat the Zombie King + complete Dr. Chen's Quest 3)

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "rebuilding." You eliminated all threats, united all forces, and mastered the key technology to control the virus. You possess an army of "lucid zombies," elite human warriors, and the cutting-edge technology left by the doctor. This is the most "perfect" ending—but "perfection" itself is a problem: you have the power to change the world; will you become the next King?

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving. Earn [Blade of Fearlessness].

### Ending Six: Flying into Space 🚀

> "You don't know if you did the right thing or the wrong thing. But at this moment, you can no longer turn back."

**Trigger Condition**: Rocket quest chain completed, choose to board the rocket yourself

**Interpretation**: This is the "lonely flight" ending. You fly into space alone, leaving behind everyone and everything. Outside the cabin is endless darkness and stars; you are like a grain of sand cast into the ocean. Maybe one day you'll find a new home, maybe not. But at least—you chose to fly.

This is the most selfish ending, and the bravest one. Selfish because you abandoned everyone; brave because you face the unknown alone.

**Endgame**: Can only restart.

### Ending Seven: Spark of Hope 🌟

> "As long as we're together, hope will never be extinguished."

**Trigger Condition**: Rocket quest chain completed, choose "Bring important people" (Miss V + Su Xiaohan + Lili Affinity all maxed at 150)

**Interpretation**: This is the "shared flight" ending. You and three companions fly into space together—Miss V grips the armrest in silence, Su Xiaohan leans on your shoulder crying, Lili wipes her eyes and suddenly laughs: "Hey, we're really going to space!"

Outside the cabin is endless darkness and stars. But inside, there is warmth, there is breath, there is each other. This is the warmest ending—not because you found a new home, but because you are together.

**Endgame**: Can only restart.

### Ending Eight: Stay Behind 🛡️

> "As long as there's a breath left, the story doesn't end."

**Trigger Condition**: Rocket quest chain completed, choose to let the doctor go

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "standing firm." You watch Dr. Chen walk up the boarding ladder step by step, his silhouette looking especially small in the rocket's shadow. He turns back and says: "Thank you. Live well... for me."

The doctor carries hope into space, while you choose to stay. The doctor leaves behind the improved antibody manufacturing device—his gift to you, and the spark he leaves for this world.

This ending is the most "heroic"—not because you did something great, but because you chose to stay.

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving.

### Ending Nine: I'm No Farming Goddess 🌾

> "In this wasteland, the greatest miracle isn't weapons or power—it's the sound of a seed breaking through the soil."

**Trigger Condition**: Complete Mu Miaomiao's Quest 3 (Expand the Experimental Field, 15 crops)

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "ordinariness." No flying into space, no becoming king, no building a new force—you just planted a field. But Miaomiao says it best: "I'm no farming goddess... I'm just an ordinary person who doesn't want anyone to go hungry anymore."

This ending deconstructs "heroism." In the apocalypse, the greatest people aren't those who wield weapons, but those who bend down to plant. Every crop says: we're still here, we still remember.

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving.

### Ending Ten: Love and Hate 💔

> "In the apocalypse, the greatest luxury isn't power or supplies—it's having someone worth hating, and someone worth loving."

**Trigger Condition**: Complete The Queen's Quest 4 (Wedding Anniversary)

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "emotion." You stand on the Castle balcony, The Queen beside you. The King built walls with power, the Leader held his ground with conviction, and The Queen stands between two walls—torn apart, yet made whole.

This ending reveals the game's deepest theme: in the apocalypse, the greatest luxury isn't survival—it's emotion. Having someone worth hating, someone worth loving—means you haven't yet become a walking corpse.

**Non-Endgame**: Can choose to continue surviving.

### Ending Eleven: Becoming the Dish 🍖

> "Your story came to an end on a dining table in some dim restaurant at the National Highway Service Area."

**Trigger Condition**: Ma San's quest chain step 4, choose "Accept"

**Interpretation**: This is the game's darkest ending. Dull pain radiates from your limbs, the iron pot bubbles away, Ma San hums a little tune—you finally understand the true source of that "mystery meat."

This ending is the punishment for "greed." Ma San lured you with the "armory" and "RPG," just as he lured hungry survivors with "mystery meat." The moment you chose "Accept," you became his prey.

**Endgame**: Can only restart.

### Ending Twelve: Elopement 🚗

> "Elope to the edge of the world, never looking back even at the end of days."

**Trigger Condition**: Liu Ruyan's quest chain step 4 (10 Gasoline to start the SUV)

**Interpretation**: This is the ending of "romance." The SUV's engine roar tears through the apocalyptic silence, Liu Ruyan sits in the passenger seat, wind pouring through the broken windows. The trunk holds only a few bottles of water, some compressed biscuits, and that crowbar—but you have each other.

"Before the apocalypse, my biggest wish was a graduation trip." — "Then this is our graduation trip."

This ending is the most powerful defiance of the apocalypse—not with weapons, not with power, but with a broken car and one person, driving toward the unknown.

**Endgame**: Can only restart.

## VIII. Hidden Clues and Subplots

### 8.1 The Mystery of the Virus's Origin

The game contains multiple hints that the zombie virus may not be naturally occurring:

1. **The Bio-Research Institute's** existence — Dr. Chen researches improved serum alone, seemingly with deep understanding of the virus
2. **The Giant Puppet** — "Puppet" implies human manufacture; guarding the aerospace base is even more suspicious
3. **The Castle's virus research** — Duke Yu Mo's Quest 2 reveals the Castle is researching the zombie virus, requiring 3x zombie gel
4. **Improved serum** — Dr. Chen can manufacture improved antibody injections, meaning the virus structure is analytically solvable
5. **The aerospace base's pristine condition** — The only intact high-tech facility in the apocalypse, implying advance preparation

These clues point to a disturbing speculation: the zombie virus may have been artificially created, and the aerospace base may be one of the virus's points of origin.

### 8.2 The King's Past

The recorder pen and the Leader's dialogue reveal The King's complete past:

1. The King was once a real estate merchant
2. In the early days of the apocalypse, he joined Haven Outpost
3. Fell in love with the Leader's sister (now The Queen)
4. The Leader didn't trust him and rejected his romance with the sister
5. After being expelled from the Outpost, The King used his business acumen to build the Castle
6. He sent female spies to infiltrate the Outpost and assassinated the Leader's intelligence operative
7. He pursues immortality with a pathological obsession with power

The King's story is one of "a rejected man's revenge"—but after succeeding, he found himself still empty.

### 8.3 The Underground Nuclear Power Device

Hidden in the Castle's underground area is a small nuclear generator; accessing it requires The King to trade the AWM for the underground key. Dismantling the generator requires a large wrench + Dimensional Storage Backpack—this is a key item for Rocket Mission 1.

The existence of the underground nuclear power device implies: the Castle possessed nuclear technology before the apocalypse. The King sealed the underground area perhaps not just to protect resources, but to hide certain secrets.

### 8.4 Yu Mo's Secret

At the Soulmate stage, Duke Yu Mo says: "There may be more than just a nuclear power device beneath the island." This line implies the island's underground may hide even greater secrets. The Castle conducts zombie virus research on the island (Quest 2), the research attracted aquatic zombies (Quest 3), and Yu Mo was forced to turn the island into a sea fortress (Quest 4). Beneath The Island's "utopian" facade lies the Castle's darkest experiments.

### 8.5 The Significance of Luluwei

Luluwei is one of the most important characters in the entire game—not for her combat ability, but because she proves the zombie virus can be reversed. Her existence is key to the "New Force" ending: improved serum can fully restore her to humanity, making her "a bridge between humans and mutants."

Luluwei's existence poses a philosophical question: if zombies can be restored to humans, is our slaughter of zombies justified?

## IX. Themes and Metaphors

### 9.1 Survival vs. Living

The game's core theme is the distinction between "survival" and "living." Survival is eating enough and not dying; living is having emotion, purpose, and dignity.

- **The Castle** chose survival—using power and resources to sustain life, but losing the soul
- **The Outpost** chose living—though poor, there is warmth between people
- **The Island** chose escapism—using consumption and entertainment to numb themselves, pretending the apocalypse doesn't exist

### 9.2 The Corruption of Power

The King went from a rejected merchant to a tyrant; the Crown Prince ending implies the player may become the next King—power is a poison, and no one is immune.

### 9.3 Loneliness and Connection

Every character battles loneliness in their own way:
- Miss V uses coldness to protect herself
- Su Xiaohan uses warmth to connect with others
- Lili uses transactions to maintain social bonds
- Mu Miaomiao uses planting to channel her longing
- The Leader uses leadership to fill the void
- Yu Mo uses loyalty to fight loneliness
- The King uses power to mask fear

The player is the only one who can connect everyone—but the price of connection is choice.

### 9.4 Seeds and Sparks

The imagery of "seeds" and "sparks" recurs throughout the game:
- Miaomiao's seeds—the continuation of life
- The Spark of Hope—the continuation of civilization
- Improved antibody injections—the continuation of knowledge

These images all point to the same theme: in the apocalypse, the most important thing isn't staying alive—it's passing things on.

### 9.5 Choices and Their Costs

Each of the game's 12 endings is the result of a choice:
- Choose power → Power Addiction
- Choose devotion → Shining Bright
- Choose solitude → Flying into Space
- Choose companions → Spark of Hope
- Choose to stand firm → Stay Behind
- Choose ordinariness → I'm No Farming Goddess
- Choose emotion → Love and Hate
- Choose greed → Becoming the Dish
- Choose romance → Elopement

There is no "correct" choice—only different costs. That is the cruelty of the apocalypse—you can't have everything, but you must choose what to give up.

### 9.6 The Mirror of the Apocalypse

Zombie World is not a story about zombies—it's a story about people. Zombies are merely the backdrop; the true protagonists are every survivor making choices in the apocalypse.

The King, the Leader, The Queen, Ma San, Miaomiao, the Doctor... they are all mirrors of the apocalypse, reflecting what human nature looks like under extreme conditions. And the player, standing at the intersection of all mirrors, sees not zombies—but themselves.
